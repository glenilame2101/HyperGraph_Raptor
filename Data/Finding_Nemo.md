# Finding Nemo (2003)

## Overview

- **Title:** Finding Nemo
- **Studio:** Pixar Animation Studios / Walt Disney Pictures
- **Director:** Andrew Stanton
- **Co-Director:** Lee Unkrich
- **Screenplay:** Andrew Stanton, Bob Peterson, David Reynolds
- **Release Date:** May 30, 2003
- **Genre:** Animated Comedy-Adventure
- **Runtime:** 100 minutes
- **Box Office:** $940.3 million worldwide

---

## Main Characters

- **Marlin** -- A clownfish and Nemo's overprotective father. After losing his wife and nearly all of his children to a barracuda attack, Marlin is consumed by fear and anxiety, smothering Nemo with rules and caution.
- **Nemo** -- Marlin's only surviving son, a young clownfish with a smaller right fin (his "lucky fin") from the barracuda attack. Brave, curious, and eager to prove himself despite his father's doubts.
- **Dory** -- A regal blue tang with severe short-term memory loss. Despite her condition, she is relentlessly optimistic, kind, and brave. She becomes Marlin's unlikely companion on his journey across the ocean.
- **Gill** -- A Moorish idol and the leader of the Tank Gang in the dentist's aquarium. Scarred from a failed escape attempt, he is determined to reach the ocean and helps Nemo find the courage to escape.
- **Bruce** -- A great white shark trying to be vegetarian. Leader of the Fish-Friendly Sharks support group. His motto: "Fish are friends, not food."
- **Crush** -- A 150-year-old green sea turtle who rides the East Australian Current (EAC) with his son Squirt. Laid-back and wise, he teaches Marlin about letting go and trusting his child.
- **Squirt** -- Crush's young son, adventurous and fearless, showing Marlin what a confident child looks like when given freedom.
- **Nigel** -- A friendly brown pelican who knows Marlin's story through the ocean grapevine and helps reunite father and son.
- **P. Sherman (Philip Sherman)** -- A scuba-diving dentist in Sydney whose office overlooks the harbor. He captures Nemo from the reef.
- **Darla** -- P. Sherman's young niece, notorious among the tank fish for shaking a previous fish to death. Nemo is meant to be her birthday present.
- **The Tank Gang** -- The fish living in the dentist's aquarium:
  - **Bloat** -- A porcupine pufferfish with a temper
  - **Peach** -- A starfish stuck to the tank glass, the lookout
  - **Jacques** -- A cleaner shrimp obsessed with cleanliness
  - **Gurgle** -- A royal gramma terrified of germs
  - **Deb (and Flo)** -- A four-striped damselfish who believes her reflection is her twin sister Flo
  - **Bubbles** -- A yellow tang obsessed with bubbles

## Plot Summary

### Prologue: The Tragedy

On the edge of the Great Barrier Reef, **Marlin** and his wife **Coral** admire their clutch of over 400 eggs nestled in their anemone home. They are joyful, picking names, dreaming of the future. Without warning, a **barracuda** attacks. Marlin is knocked unconscious. When he wakes, Coral and nearly every egg are gone. Only one tiny, damaged egg remains. Marlin cradles it and names him **Nemo**, the name Coral had loved. From this moment, Marlin vows to never let anything happen to his son.

### Act 1: The Capture

Years later, Nemo is a young clownfish starting his first day of school. Marlin is a nervous wreck, rattling off dangers and smothering Nemo with warnings. At the school drop-off at the edge of the reef, Nemo meets other young fish and their more relaxed parents, highlighting just how overprotective Marlin is.

When the class takes a field trip to the Drop-Off -- the wall where the reef ends and the open ocean begins -- Nemo is dared by his classmates to swim out to a boat anchored in the deep water. Frustrated by his father's constant hovering and desperate to prove he's capable, Nemo defiantly swims out and touches the boat. But before he can swim back, a **scuba diver** -- **P. Sherman**, a dentist from Sydney -- scoops Nemo into a bag and takes him away.

Marlin chases the boat desperately but can't keep up. As the boat speeds away, he catches a glimpse of a diver's mask falling to the ocean floor, with an address written on it: **P. Sherman, 42 Wallaby Way, Sydney**.

### Act 2: Two Journeys

The film follows two parallel stories:

#### Marlin and Dory's Journey Across the Ocean

Marlin races into the open ocean, far beyond anything he has ever known. Almost immediately, he collides with **Dory**, a regal blue tang who claims she knows which way the boat went. But Dory has short-term memory loss -- she forgets things almost as quickly as she learns them. Despite her condition, she becomes Marlin's partner on the journey, and her optimism and fearlessness complement his anxiety and caution.

Together, they face a gauntlet of ocean dangers:

- **The Sharks:** They encounter **Bruce**, **Anchor** (a hammerhead), and **Chum** (a mako), great white sharks running a support group to stop eating fish. Things go well until Marlin finds the diver's mask and a drop of blood from Dory's nose sends Bruce into a feeding frenzy. Marlin and Dory barely escape when a torpedo from a sunken submarine detonates old naval mines.

- **The Anglerfish:** In the deep ocean trench, they're lured by the light of a monstrous anglerfish. In the chaos, Dory -- who can read -- deciphers the address on the mask: **P. Sherman, 42 Wallaby Way, Sydney**. This becomes her anchor phrase, the one thing she remembers.

- **The Moonfish School:** A school of moonfish, impressed by Marlin's bravery, gives them directions but warns them not to swim through the trench -- to swim over it instead. Marlin, impatient and skeptical, ignores the advice and swims through the trench, where they encounter a forest of **jellyfish**. Dory treats them like a game at first, but both are badly stung. Marlin, using the last of his strength, carries Dory out before losing consciousness.

- **The East Australian Current (EAC):** They wake up riding the EAC on the back of **Crush**, the ancient, laid-back sea turtle. Crush and his son **Squirt** show Marlin a different philosophy of parenting. When Squirt tumbles out of the current, Crush resists the urge to intervene, letting the little turtle find his own way back. "You never really know, but when they know, you know, you know?" Crush tells Marlin. It's a turning point -- Marlin begins to understand that protecting Nemo from everything isn't the same as being a good father.

While riding the current, Marlin tells the turtles the story of his quest. The story passes from turtle to sea horse to dolphin to pelican, spreading across the entire ocean like wildfire, eventually reaching **Nigel**, a pelican in Sydney Harbour who frequents the dentist's office window.

- **The Whale:** After exiting the EAC, Marlin and Dory are swallowed by a **blue whale**. Marlin panics, but Dory, who claims she can "speak whale," tells Marlin the whale is telling them to let go. Marlin, who has spent the entire journey -- and his entire life -- holding on too tight, finally lets go. The whale blows them out through its blowhole into Sydney Harbour.

#### Nemo and the Tank Gang

Meanwhile, Nemo finds himself in a fish tank in P. Sherman's dental office at **42 Wallaby Way, Sydney**, overlooking the harbour. He meets the **Tank Gang**, a group of fish who dream of freedom. Their leader, **Gill**, is a scarred Moorish idol who was also taken from the ocean. Gill sees himself in Nemo and devises an escape plan.

**The Plan:** Nemo must jam the tank's filter with a pebble, forcing the dentist to remove the fish and put them in plastic bags to clean the tank. Once in bags, they'll roll off the counter, out the window, across the road, and into the harbour.

Nemo's first attempt fails -- the filter nearly kills him, and Gill, wracked with guilt, calls off the plan. But when Nigel arrives at the window with news that Marlin has crossed the entire ocean looking for his son, Nemo is inspired. His father, the most timid fish on the reef, has faced sharks, jellyfish, and a whale for him. Nemo tries again and succeeds in jamming the filter.

### Act 3: Reunion

The filter breaks down and the tank grows filthy, but the dentist installs a high-tech new filter before the fish can be bagged, foiling the escape plan. On the day **Darla** arrives for her birthday, the tank is spotless and the fish are trapped.

In the harbour, Marlin and Dory are scooped up by Nigel, who flies them to the dentist's window. Inside, the dentist is about to give Nemo to Darla. Nemo, having learned from Gill, plays dead (floating belly-up), hoping the dentist will flush him down the toilet and into the ocean. But Marlin arrives at the window and sees Nemo floating motionless. He doesn't realize it's an act. He believes his son is dead.

Devastated, Marlin thanks Dory for everything but tells her he has to go home alone. Dory begs him to stay -- "When I look at you, I'm home. Please don't go. I remember things better with you. I do." But Marlin leaves. It's the emotional low point of the film.

Back in the office, the dentist scoops Nemo up to give to Darla, but Gill launches himself onto the dentist's tray and catapults Nemo into the spit sink, which drains to the ocean. Nemo slides down the pipes and into Sydney Harbour.

In the harbour, Dory, now alone and confused, encounters Nemo but doesn't recognize him -- she's already forgetting. Then she reads the word "Sydney" on a pipe, and the memory floods back: **P. Sherman, 42 Wallaby Way, Sydney**. She remembers everything. She realizes the little clownfish in front of her is Nemo and excitedly leads him to find Marlin.

They catch Marlin just as he's swimming away. Father and son are reunited. But the moment is cut short when Dory and a school of grouper fish are caught in a massive fishing net being hauled to the surface. Nemo wants to help, but Marlin's instinct is to hold him back, to protect him. Then Marlin makes the hardest choice of his life: he lets Nemo go.

Nemo swims into the net and tells the fish to **swim down** together. Hundreds of fish swim downward in unison, and the net breaks free from the trawler. Dory and the fish are saved. Marlin finds Nemo lying still in the settling debris and fears the worst, but Nemo is fine. They embrace.

### Epilogue

Back home on the reef, life has changed. Marlin is a neighborhood celebrity, and his incredible journey has made him braver and more relaxed. He lets Nemo swim to school with his friends, giving him freedom and trust. Dory remains their close companion and neighbor. Mr. Ray the teacher takes the kids on another field trip, and this time Marlin waves goodbye with a smile instead of fear.

In a post-credits scene, the Tank Gang has finally made it to Sydney Harbour, still sealed in their plastic bags. They bob in the water triumphantly -- they've escaped. Then comes the realization: "Now what?"

## Themes

- **Letting go and trust:** The core of the film. Marlin's arc is about learning that he cannot protect Nemo from every danger, and that trying to do so only holds Nemo back. True love means trusting those you love to face the world.
- **Overcoming fear:** Marlin is defined by his fear after losing Coral. His journey forces him to confront every terror the ocean holds, and by doing so, he rediscovers his own courage.
- **Disability and capability:** Nemo's lucky fin and Dory's memory loss are presented as challenges, not limitations. Both characters repeatedly prove that their conditions do not define their worth or abilities. Nemo's fin doesn't stop him from being brave; Dory's memory loss doesn't stop her from being the most loyal and emotionally intelligent character in the film.
- **The power of community:** Marlin cannot complete his journey alone. Every step is aided by others -- Dory, Crush, Nigel, the moonfish, even Bruce. Similarly, Nemo survives because of the Tank Gang's solidarity.
- **Parenting and independence:** Crush and Squirt serve as the thematic counterpoint to Marlin and Nemo. Crush's relaxed approach -- letting Squirt learn from experience -- shows Marlin that children grow stronger when given room to fail and try again.
- **Persistence and hope:** Dory's philosophy of "just keep swimming" is the film's emotional backbone. Even when everything seems lost, the simple act of continuing forward is what saves them.

## Soundtrack and Score

- **Composer:** Thomas Newman
- Newman's score is atmospheric and shimmering, using unconventional instruments and ambient textures to evoke the vast, mysterious world beneath the waves. The score was nominated for the **Academy Award for Best Original Score**.
- **"Beyond the Sea"** by Robbie Williams plays over the end credits, a fitting nod to the film's oceanic adventure.

## Awards and Recognition

- **Academy Award for Best Animated Feature** (2004)
- Nominated for **Best Original Screenplay**, **Best Original Score**, and **Best Sound Editing**
- At the time of its release, it was the **highest-grossing animated film ever**, earning $940.3 million worldwide
- Holds a **99% approval rating** on Rotten Tomatoes
- Selected for preservation in the **National Film Registry** by the Library of Congress in 2021

## Production Details

- Director Andrew Stanton was inspired to make the film after a trip to an aquarium with his son and realizing he was so worried about the boy's safety that he wasn't letting either of them enjoy the experience.
- The Pixar team took scuba diving lessons, marine biology courses, and studied oceanography extensively to accurately portray underwater environments.
- The film required developing entirely new technology for rendering water, light refraction, and the movement of aquatic life. The underwater environments in the film were a massive leap forward in animation technology.
- After the film's release, clownfish sales surged worldwide, leading marine biologists to express concern about wild populations being captured for aquariums -- an ironic outcome for a film about setting a captive fish free.

## Legacy

*Finding Nemo* is widely considered one of Pixar's greatest masterpieces and one of the finest animated films ever made. It dominated the box office in 2003 and became a cultural phenomenon. Dory's phrase "just keep swimming" entered the popular lexicon as a mantra for perseverance.

The film spawned a sequel, **Finding Dory** (2016), which follows Dory as she searches for her own parents and explores her backstory with the same emotional depth. Finding Dory grossed over $1 billion worldwide.

The film also inspired **The Seas with Nemo & Friends**, an attraction at EPCOT in Walt Disney World, and **Turtle Talk with Crush**, an interactive show featuring real-time animation.

*Finding Nemo* endures because its story is universal: a parent's love, a child's need for independence, and the courage it takes to let go. Beneath its dazzling ocean visuals and sharp humor lies one of cinema's most poignant meditations on fear, loss, and the bravery of simply moving forward.
